IoT Crop Box

Sep 2023 - present


Objective: The primary objective of this project is to implement automation and precision in cultivating optimal growth conditions for plants. Through continuous adjustments facilitated by machine learning algorithms, the aim is to achieve the most favorable outcomes for plant development, minimizing the requirement for extensive human intervention. The overarching goal is to establish and manage multiple crop boxes concurrently, each capable of cultivating various crops. This will be orchestrated by a centralized control system, ensuring systematic monitoring and optimization to guarantee the robust growth of all plants.


Background: Amidst the backdrop of the Covid-19 pandemic, Singapore underwent a significant transformation in its approach to agriculture. This transformation was driven by the recognition of the need to achieve self-sustainability in agriculture, given the constrictions imposed on import and export activities. This shift has encouraged a determined ambition for Singapore to achieve self-sufficiency by the year 2030.


Problem Statement:

Farming faces challenges in water management and efficient irrigation practices which leads to suboptimal water usage, reduced crop yields and increased operational costs. One critical issue is the inadequate monitoring of soil moisture level which contributes to water wastage and escalates the expenses associated with manpower. To address such challenges in agriculture, we propose the implementation of an IoT-based smart water irrigation system. This IoT solution aims to conserve precious water resources, optimize crop growth, and ensure food security by providing real-time soil moisture monitoring and intelligent irrigation control. The cultivation of non-native crops in Singapore poses a significant challenge due to the unsuitability of our local climate. With the use of an intelligent irrigation control system, the cultivation of both native and non-native crops is possible by adjusting the controlled environment to their unique requirements. This project aims to reduce the burden of labor costs on farmers while promoting efficient water usage and enhancing agricultural productivity.


Components used:

1. Capacitive Soil Moisture Sensor

Soil moisture sensor serves as the foundation of the irrigation system. An electrical conductivity soil moisture sensor uses electrical conductivity to measure the soil moisture, the smaller the resistance in the soil means that the soil is wet and higher the resistance means the soil is wet.

2. Temperature & Humidity Sensor (DHT22)

Temperature and humidity sensors play a crucial role in regulating the controlled environment. These sensors continuously monitor the temperature and humidity levels, allowing the system to make the necessary adjustments to meet the distinct requirements of different crops, including non-native crops within Singapore’s climate. By maintaining temperature and humidity within the optimal bounds, the system creates the ideal environment for robust plant growth.

3. Water Level Sensor

Water level sensor is the eyes in an IoT-based smart irrigation system. It measures the liquid level in the system for irrigation control. An optical water level sensor uses infrared LEDs and phototransistors, and when the sensor is in the air, they are optically coupled. When the sensor head is immersed in the liquid, the infrared light will escape, causing the output to change. These sensors can detect the presence or absence of almost any liquid. When the water tank level falls lower than a predetermined threshold, the water level sensor would signal for water replenishment. This real-time data update will ensure that the system never runs out of water for the irrigation cycles.

4. LED Strips

Light is a crucial element in a plant's growth and the LED strips play an important part. It ensures that the plant has sufficient energy to grow and at the same time, helps to increase temperature in the crop box. Currently, the crop box is only set up with pure white LED lights, but in the future, it will be amended to have RGB colours to have the optimised plant growth.

5. Fans

Fans, though small, play an important role in ensuring air movement in the box, as well as allowing fresh air to enter and hot air to escape. This ensures that the air in the box does not go stale, allowing bacterias or fungus to grow easily.

6. Water Foggers

Water fogger is used for humidity regulation where fine water droplets are released into the air to increase humidity level when needed. It also prevents moisture stress in plants, ensuring that they thrive in a moisture-balanced environment.

7. Water Pump and Sprinkler

Water pumps are responsible for delivering water to the sprinklers. The pump is synchronized with data from soil moisture level sensor. When the irrigation system determines that it is time to water the plant, the water pump activates pressurizing the water supply from the tank to distribute efficiently to the sprinklers. The sprinkler will disperse the water evenly and ensures a uniform coverage of moisture for the entire cultivation area.

8. ARDUINO UNO WIFI REV2

The ARDUINO is the brain of the whole system, connecting the hardware to the software and vice versa through digital and analog signals. This will be replaced with ESP32 in the future for the development stage.

A major factor in making considerable contributions to food production is an irrigation system's effectiveness. Freshwater surplus and waste can only be observed visually in the most severe instances of poor management, but any overabundance leads to high water demand and frequently contributes to the loss of nutrients from the soil. To be able to deliver the right amount of water to the crops when and where it is needed, based on the requested amount calculated for each crop, irrigation management systems for farmers need to be carefully developed. As a result, by utilizing IoT and machine learning scenarios, an effective monitoring and control system for agricultural irrigation through mobile and web applications can be developed with high accuracy, resulting in significant water, energy, and labor savings. Additionally, machine learning is essential for irrigation prediction in order to support farmer-based irrigation decisions. Since mobile phones are now so widely used and practically every person carries one in their pocket, farmers are also using software programs in the form of apps, either for information-sharing or for agro-advisory services.
